Evidence-Based Leadership

Leadership Portfolio

This portfolio documents selected leadership work from my time as Vice Principal at Colegio Panamericano in Colombia. It is not meant to be a collection of polished claims. It is evidence of practical school leadership.

Instructional Leadership and Teacher Support

Formal and informal observations, teacher feedback, coaching, summative evaluations, and practical support with classroom management, lesson structure, pacing, engagement, routines, and organization.

AP Alignment and Academic Systems

Created an AP Grades vs. AP Exam Results tracker to compare classroom grades with external AP exam performance and support more data-informed academic follow-up.

Responsible AI Leadership

Helped lead the AI Committee and contributed to approved AI language for student responsibilities, teacher responsibilities, the Acceptable Use Policy, and the Academic Integrity Policy.

New Teacher Onboarding and Retention

Worked with HR to improve new teacher onboarding, including arrival support, apartment readiness, foreign teacher cheat sheets, and practical transition systems.

Organizational Systems

Supported course catalog development, HoD meeting structures, exam procedures, proctoring expectations, unit planner follow-up, syllabi, AP Fair organization, student surveys, and grade-level transition work.

Faculty Feedback and Growth

Faculty feedback showed strengths in approachability, support, genuine interest, positive energy, and willingness to help, while also pointing to growth areas in visibility, communication, follow-through, and trust-building.

Research and Writing

Teacher Retention, Leadership, and School Culture

My PhD in Educational Leadership focused on teacher retention. That research continues to shape how I think about onboarding, teacher support, leadership trust, workload, school culture, and the conditions that make educators stay or leave.

I am also developing a book project on the teacher retention crisis, drawing from research, leadership experience, and real stories from schools.
